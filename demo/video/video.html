Session 222: Applying a Novel Approach "Problem Frames" to Industrial Software Requirements Analysis
Jie Sun, PhD Candidate, Zhejiang University
Thu Oct 20th 14:30-15:30 | Hall B
SLIDES Applying a Novel Approach "Problem Frames" to Industrial Software Requirements Analysis - Jie Sun (PDF, [SIZE]B)
VIDEO Applying a Novel Approach "Problem Frames" to Industrial Software Requirements Analysis - Jie Sun (MP4, [SIZE]MB, [00:HH:MM])
Problem Frames (PFs) is a promising approach for software requirements modeling and analysis. By focusing on problems rather than solutions, PFs drive developers to explicitly analyze and describe requirements problems in full detail. Yet, to date, this approach has attracted more attention in the academic community than in the software development industry, due to the difficulty of deriving PFs from a complex real-world problem context. To overcome this difficulty, we provide a systematic and practical approach to derive individual PFs from a complex problem. To illustrate this approach, we also introduce some real world examples extracted from an equity trading problem.

Jie Sun received her BSc degree in computer science from Zhejiang University in 2008. She is currently a PhD student in the College of Computer Science, Zhejiang University. Her research interests include requirements engineering, distributed software architecture and business process modeling.
 
Session 223: Supporting Lean with Project Benchmark and Estimation
Bin Xu, R&D Group, State Street Hangzhou
Thu Oct 20th 14:30-15:30 | Hall C
SLIDES Supporting Lean with Project Benchmark and Estimation - Bin Xu (PDF, [SIZE]B)
VIDEO Supporting Lean with Project Benchmark and Estimation - Bin Xu (MP4, [SIZE]MB, [00:HH:MM])
The State Street Standard Estimating and Tracking Practice supports many of Lean’s guiding principles including elimination of waste, amplifying learning, seeing the whole and so on. This presentation introduces a transparent estimation process using five objective metrics readily available today by all project teams. Using this process and automating it through tools increases the communication level between all stakeholders and allows both business and IT to focus on the tradeoffs between scope, effort/cost, time, and quality early in the lifecycle. The benefits to this approach eliminates waste in development, increases stakeholder understanding of what is being built, and provides teams and management objective views of the whole system based on past performance using objective metrics.

Session 233: Measure and monitor uncertainties through software project progress
Xiaochun Zhu, QA Manager, State Street Hangzhou and Hengtian
Thu Oct 20th 15:30-16:30 | Hall C
SLIDES Measure and monitor uncertainties through software project progress - Xiaochun Zhu (PDF, [SIZE]B)
VIDEO Measure and monitor uncertainties through software project progress - Xiaochun Zhu (MP4, [SIZE]MB, [00:HH:MM])
Software projects always suffer from kinds of uncertain factors. As a common strategy, contingency buffer is added to the effort estimate. So how to assess the uncertainty and monitor the contingency buffer during the project progress is always a hot topic in software management . In this paper, we propose an earned-value approach to assess and monitor software uncertainty. It combines the value-at-risk method in financial field and the earned-value-feedback process of earned value project management, and proposes a framework which contains value-at-uncertainty system, consumed-value-feedback system and experienced base to support in-process uncertainty measurement and contingency buffer management. Value-at-uncertainty system is used to assess the uncertainty in progress of a project, and consumed-value-feedback system is applied to provide the decision support of how to deal with the buffer at that specific time. A case study in software test execution of 24 projects is conducted to evaluate the approach. With the approach, both the accuracy and the effectiveness of uncertainty assessment can be improved along with project execution progress.

Xiaochun Zhu,Joined State Street Technology Center in Jun 2004, and has been worked in State Street Global Markets for 7+ years. Leading and managing testing and SQA activities for 50+ production releases of 10+ foreign exchange products. Joined Insigma Hengtian at Jan 2011, working as Hengtian Account Managers of GM and GS, Manager of QA department. Received PhD degree of Zhejiang University at Dec 2010 with the specialty Computer Science and Technology, focusing on software testing and software effort estimation.
